# PowerTune Research Study: Intel Evo Ultrabook Efficiency

**Target Device**: Dell XPS 13 (9315)
**Processor**: Intel Core i7-1250U
**PowerTune Profile**: `silent.yaml`

## 📊 Baseline Observations (Out of the Box)
- **Idle Drain Rate**: 5.8W - 7.2W
- **Active Background Noise**: 142 processes
- **Timer Resolution**: 1.0ms (Forced by `msedge.exe` background tabs)
- **CPU C-State Residency**: 62% in C10

## 🕵️ PowerTune Diagnostics
The `analyzers/timers.py` module identified that **Microsoft Edge** was holding a high-resolution 1ms timer even when minimized. This prevented the Intel i7-1250U from entering its lowest power C10 state consistently.

Additionally, `vendor/dell.ps1` flagged **Dell SupportAssist** as running a background hardware scan every 2 hours, spiking power draw to 24W for 5-minute intervals.

## 🛠️ Applied Optimizations
1.  **Timer Resolution Reset**: Forced system-wide 15.6ms timer resolution.
2.  **Dell Bloat Suspension**: Disabled `DellOptimizer` and `SupportAssist` background telemetry.
3.  **ASPM Enforcement**: Forced Active State Power Management (ASPM) for the PCIe NVMe drive.

## ✅ Final Results
- **Optimized Idle Drain Rate**: **3.2W - 4.1W**
- **CPU C-State Residency**: **94% in C10**
- **Battery Life Projection**: Increased from 8.5 hours to **14.2 hours** (+67%).
